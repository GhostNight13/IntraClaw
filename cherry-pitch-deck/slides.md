**Visual notes:** Icons should be custom line-art, not stock. Each column same width. Cherry-red accent line above each icon. No illustrations of handshakes or gears — too generic.

**Headline:**

**Supporting copy:**

>
> **Pet Spend Rising** — US pet care spend grew from $136B (2022) to projected $186B (2026). Elective and preventive categories grew fastest; these are the declined tickets.
>
> **BNPL is Default Behavior** — 39% of US consumers used BNPL in 2024. Under-35 owners expect "pay over time" at the vet the same way they expect it at Sephora.

**Speaker notes:**
> The reason VetTiers works in 2026 and wouldn't have worked in 2020 is three shifts stacked.
>
> First, the AVMA released Spectrum of Care guidance telling vets it's no longer ethical to present only the gold-standard treatment — you're supposed to offer accessible alternatives. That's a green light for tiered pricing that didn't exist before.
>
> Second, US pet spend is projected to hit $186 billion this year, and the fastest-growing categories are exactly the ones that get declined at the front desk — dental, elective surgery, preventive.
>
> Third, BNPL is no longer novel. Under-35 pet owners — who are now the majority of new clients — land at the front desk already expecting to see a "four payments" button. If you don't offer it, they assume you're behind the times.
>

## Slide 9 — Integration Architecture

**Layout:** Diagram-heavy. A clean left-to-right flow diagram: VetTiers front-desk UI → VetTiers backend → Cherry Partner API → Cherry-hosted compliance surfaces (iframe or redirect for SSN/APR). Minimal text.

**Visual notes:** Use the FigJam-style diagram aesthetic but cleaner. Boxes in off-white with 1px borders. Arrows in Cherry red. Label each arrow with the payload type ("loan application intent" / "pre-qual result" / "funding event webhook"). Compliance surfaces are visually walled off with a dashed "Cherry-controlled zone" boundary.

**Headline:**
> **Our UI surfaces your API. Your compliance surfaces stay yours.**

**Supporting copy:**
> 1. **VetTiers front desk** sends loan intent to our backend.
> 2. **VetTiers backend** calls Cherry Partner API with clinic + transaction context.
> 3. **Cherry-hosted iframe/redirect** collects SSN, runs soft pull, returns pre-qual result.
> 4. **VetTiers UI** renders the monthly payment, e-signs the estimate, books the procedure.
> 5. **Cherry webhook** fires funding event → we mark the estimate as financed in our system.

**Speaker notes:**
> Here's the integration, five steps, no magic.
>
> Step one: the vet tech on our front-desk UI triggers a "finance this estimate" action. Our backend gets the intent.
>
> Step two: our backend calls your Partner API with the clinic ID, merchant ID, loan amount, and customer contact info. Nothing regulated crosses our boundary.
>
> Step three: your hosted surface — whether that's an iframe, a redirect, a mobile SDK — takes over for the parts that need to be yours. SSN collection, APR disclosure, consent flow. We never see those fields.
>
> Step four: we receive back the pre-qualified terms, render the monthly payment in our UI in your approved language, and collect the owner's e-signature on the estimate.
>
> Step five: your webhook tells us when the loan funds. We mark the estimate financed, the clinic's accounting system gets the update, and we close the loop.
>
> This is a two-week integration sprint against your sandbox. Not a two-quarter rewrite.

**Sources/citations:** Cherry public Partner API documentation.

## Slide 10 — Compliance Posture

**Layout:** 4 compliance principles in a 2x2 grid. Each quadrant is a card with a title, one sentence, and a checkmark. Minimal.

**Visual notes:** Each card has a thin red top border. Icons optional — if used, they should be line-art lock/shield/document/scale. No clip art.

**Headline:**
> **What we don't touch is as important as what we do.**

**Supporting copy:**
> **TILA / Regulation Z** — All APR, finance charge, and payment-schedule disclosures render in Cherry's hosted surface. Our UI only displays Cherry-approved language.
>
> **SSN & regulated PII** — Collected, stored, and transmitted exclusively by Cherry. Our database contains no SSN field.
>
> **No direct underwriting** — We make no credit decisions. We pass intent to Cherry; Cherry decides.
>
> **Merchant agreement flow-through** — Each clinic signs Cherry's merchant agreement directly. We facilitate, we do not re-paper.

**Speaker notes:**
> Compliance is the area most likely to kill a partnership, so I want to be explicit.
>
> TILA, Reg Z, Reg B, all the lending consumer protection rules — we do not touch them. Every disclosure renders in your hosted surface, every piece of required pre-contract language is your language, not ours. My UI can display "as low as $62/month" only if your compliance team approves that phrasing for pre-qualification marketing.
>
> We store no SSN. Not a hash, not a last-four. Our database schema literally does not have the column. If a data-breach headline hits someday, it won't be a "vet platform leaks credit PII" story because we never had it.
>
> We never underwrite. We never run a credit model. We pass loan intent, you return a decision, we render it.
>
> Each clinic signs your merchant agreement directly — we just route the onboarding. Our revenue comes from a flat SaaS fee to the clinic, not a piece of your merchant fee. No misaligned incentive.

**Sources/citations:** TILA 15 USC §1601; Reg Z 12 CFR §1026; Cherry public merchant terms.
