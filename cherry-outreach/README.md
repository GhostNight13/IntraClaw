## Signal reading — what response means what

### GREEN (proceed to build at full speed)
- Any reply from Cherry that names a concrete next step: sandbox access, NDA to sign, intro to a specific integrations PM, or a scheduled call.
- "Come back at N clinics / $M in waitlist" with a concrete N and an open door. This is a yes with a gate — treat as green.
- A LinkedIn reply from Pramod engaging on the tier-prequalification product question. Even non-committal engagement = green because it validates the thesis and opens a back-channel.

### YELLOW (keep building, dual-track Scratchpay quietly)
- A generic "we'll pass your info along" autoresponse with no human follow-up within 10 days.
- A "not now, not a fit right now" without a gate or re-engagement offer.
- LinkedIn DMs marked "seen" but zero replies after 14 days across all three.
- Any reply that redirects to a self-serve merchant signup (which requires a live practice, not a software platform) — this means Cherry doesn't currently have a platform-partner track, which is real information but not a close.

### RED (shift primary plan to Scratchpay + reassess product)
- Explicit "we do not work with pre-revenue software platforms" — full stop.
- Explicit "we only integrate via [named incumbent PIMS]" — means Cherry's integration strategy is aggregator-first, and VetTiers as a standalone layer won't fit.
- No reply on any channel by day 45 AND no reply to the week-2 bump. Full silence at 45 days is a soft no.

## Response adaptation playbook

### If Cherry responds POSITIVELY (green signal)
1. Reply within 24 hours — faster if the reply came from a named person.
2. Do NOT send the 3-minute Loom until they ask for it. Gate the reveal.
3. For the intro call: come with (a) the live waitlist number, (b) the tier-prequalification question already written, (c) 3 specific design-partner clinics who will vouch for VetTiers on request, (d) a one-page compliance posture.
4. Pause the Scratchpay fallback (#06). Do not cross-shop in the open.
5. Ask on the call: "What's the smallest possible first step — sandbox keys against a sample merchant?" Anchor them to a lightweight commitment.

### If Cherry responds NEGATIVELY or with a volume gate
1. Thank them, acknowledge the gate explicitly, and ask: "What does 'ready' look like concretely — number of live clinics, $ of originated volume, or a specific PIMS integration already shipped?"
2. Get the gate in writing. Then build to it, visibly, and re-open at 75% of the number (not 100% — earlier is better for mindshare).
3. Send Scratchpay (#06) the day after the Cherry "not yet" reply, no earlier. Not in parallel — Cherry will notice if they later run references.

### If Cherry goes SILENT
1. Week 2: send #05 in-thread only.
2. Week 4: one final LinkedIn note to Felix Steinmeyer (CEO) — founder to founder, 60 words max, "I've tried three channels, happy to be told no, but want to make sure I'm not shouting into the void." [Not pre-drafted because it should be truly personal if it's sent at all.]
3. Week 6: trigger #06 (Scratchpay).
4. Do not burn down the Cherry door further. Founders talk. A reputation for gracefully accepting silence is worth more than one aggressive follow-up.

## Rules I am not allowed to break

- No fabricated numbers. Every [TODO] in the emails must be replaced with a real figure before sending.
- No fake urgency ("we're closing the round next week"). Ayman is bootstrapped — lying about that destroys the pitch's only moat, which is honesty.
- No quoting Cherry's fees, approval rates, or terms as if guaranteed. The public-facing "~90% approval" and "up to $35K" language is Cherry's own marketing — it's safe to quote. Anything beyond that = do not invent.
- No parallel sending to Cherry and Scratchpay. Sequential only. If both respond positively someday, that's a luxury problem — solve it then.
- No cold-call to Cherry. The partnerships inbox + LinkedIn DMs are the civilized path. Calling a fintech out of the blue burns goodwill.
